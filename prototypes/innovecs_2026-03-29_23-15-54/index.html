Public signals behind the prototype

The prototype is anchored to Innovecs' own supply-chain language and recent public logistics discussion, not generic mobile wishlists.

Signal 01 Innovecs markets supply-chain software around WMS and API/EDI integrations, document flows, and operational scale. The mobile gap is therefore commercial as much as technical.
Signal 02 Their 4PL EDI case study talks about inconsistent document processing, delayed responses, and limited visibility. A mobile exception layer directly improves those moments.
Signal 03 The AI supply-chain page highlights check-ins, milestone tracking, truck queues, detention fees, and approval bottlenecks. Those are live workflows, not back-office reports.
Signal 04 Voice workflows already promise 30%+ productivity improvement and 99.9% pick precision. That says frontline UX matters, which makes mobile a natural next layer.
Signal 05 No relevant first-party public Innovecs app appeared in US Apple or Google store checks on 2026-03-29. The opportunity is still open to define.
Signal 06 For a COO, this is not a design refresh. It is a tighter product package for delivery teams, pilots, demos, and operator adoption.

What Elinext can actually ship

A practical first release does not try to mobilize the entire platform. It starts with the exceptions that cost the most time and credibility.

1
Dock Arrival Workflow Capture license plate, trailer, carrier, bay, and arrival time from one screen.
2
Document AI Intake Scan POD, bill of lading, or receipt and turn it into structured fields with confidence markers.
3
Structured Exception Routing Reassign bays, add reason codes, escalate to support, or request manager approval without leaving the thread.
4
Unified Mobile Timeline Keep the operator, dispatcher, and support agent aligned on one live record.
