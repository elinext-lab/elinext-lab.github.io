Our Read

The service-management hire tells us lbase is not just shipping features. It is scaling support across TMS, driver apps, addHelix services, and new eCMR flows. That mix creates many exception paths: route changes, POD issues, offline sync, and document handoffs. When those paths stay manual, dispatch loses time, tickets bounce, and product work slows. The risk is not missing ideas. It is operational friction in every release.

What We’d Do

We would place one dedicated squad under your PO or service lead.

01

Map the real service path

Trace lbase, addHelix, driver, and eCMR handoffs. Find the repeat incident paths that drain support time and slow release decisions.

02

Expose route and POD failures early

Build one service view for route changes, proof gaps, unreadable document uploads, and slow sync events before those issues become ticket ping-pong.

03

Tighten release quality around execution flows

Add focused QA and regression checks for driver actions, offline behavior, status updates, signatures, and freight-document edge cases.

04

Turn service work into product evidence

Instrument adoption, failure reasons, and slow screens across web and mobile surfaces, so roadmap calls come from data, not anecdotes.
