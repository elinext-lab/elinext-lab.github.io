Our read

The hard part is not just hiring

Your hiring signal points to a bigger issue than filling two roles. It suggests TekStaff is scaling data standards, training, and change work across live client programs. Your public flow still splits work between website forms, external job-board handoff, and contractor documents. When those handoffs stay manual, delivery teams lose time, leaders lose trust in the numbers, and change plans slow down.

What we'd do

A short scoped project around one clean operating layer

Engagement model

We would start with a scoped eight-week project, with weekly demos and a handover plan for the wider rollout.

Team shape

1 solution lead, 1 data engineer, 1 full-stack engineer, 1 business analyst, and 1 QA and change analyst.

Relevant stack

Python Node.js React .NET
01

Map the handoffs

Map the data path across client intake, current openings, contractor documents, and TPS payroll events. Set one field model and one owner for each handoff.

02

Build one operating layer

Build a thin operations layer for recruiter, payroll, and delivery teams, so candidate status, exceptions, and approvals stop living in separate places.

03

Give leaders clean reporting

Create simple dashboards for submission speed, payroll blockers, training completion, and delivery risk. Give Dean's team clean definitions before wider reporting starts.

04

Roll out with change support

Run a pilot with one delivery stream, add short training packs, and fix adoption gaps weekly before the process expands.
