Our read

Your open role points to a bigger move: MeetingPackage is turning integrations into a growth engine. The product already connects CRS, Booking Engine, Channel Manager, Salesforce, Cvent, Mews, Opera, and partner APIs. The hard part is not one connector. It is keeping data mapping, webhooks, retries, and legacy PHP paths stable while new partners arrive. If that load sits on four engineers, partner deals can slow and customer teams feel the delay.

What we'd do

We would run a dedicated integrations squad under your Product Owner or PM. The goal is extra senior capacity without changing how your team makes product calls.

Team shape

  • Tech Lead
  • 2 Senior Engineers
  • QA Automation
  • DevOps support
1

Map the live flow

Map the current partner flow, API contracts, failure cases, and legacy PHP touchpoints before adding new connector work.

2

Build reusable patterns

Build reusable connector patterns for bookings, RFPs, contacts, availability, and status updates across Salesforce, Cvent, Mews, and Opera.

3

Automate regression checks

Add automated integration tests with mocked third-party APIs, webhook replay, contract checks, and clear failure alerts for support.

4

Ship a pilot sprint

Ship one high-value connector or hardening sprint first, then demo progress each week with your product owner.
