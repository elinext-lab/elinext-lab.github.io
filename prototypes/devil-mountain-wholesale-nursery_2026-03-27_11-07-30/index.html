Public evidence

The pain is already on Devil Mountain's own site

This concept is not based on guesswork. The current ordering pages directly describe the inventory, transfer, and account-access friction the team works around today.

Inventory changes minute to minute

The FAQ explicitly says the catalog is the most accurate source available, but staff still need to verify items in the yard before orders are trusted.

Transfers take real time

Nearby transfers can take one to two days, while statewide movement can take much longer. That makes fulfillment path and ETA quality a commercial issue, not just an ops issue.

Account access is still manual

Online access is not automated. Staff verify account-holder status manually, then route users through Xecurify setup. That is visible workflow fragmentation.

Best wedge: do not sell Aaron Ryan a generic redesign. Show a mobile inventory and transfer control layer that reduces verification calls, shortens substitute decisions, and makes the existing catalog feel operationally trustworthy across all 15 locations.

Prototype focus

Four changes that matter to procurement and branch ops

The goal is not a bigger feature list. The goal is faster certainty when an order depends on multiple yards, live stock risk, or spec substitutions.

01
Confidence-scored inventory by yard

Show count, freshness of verification, and whether the stock is ready to reserve or needs a spot check.

02
Transfer ETA before commitment

Surface the best nearby lane, statewide alternative, and dispatch timing before the customer gets a promise.

03
Substitute recommendations with ops logic

Rank alternatives by spec fit, delivery speed, margin sensitivity, and local stock strength.

04
Role-based approvals on the phone

Let buyers, sales leads, and branch managers resolve exceptions without waiting for another inbox loop.

05
Project-list visibility instead of item-by-item friction

See the whole job's risk, not just one SKU at a time, so procurement decisions happen earlier.

06
Cleaner bridge over the current stack

The concept sits on top of the existing commerce and account systems instead of requiring a full rebuild.

How the decision flow changes

Devil Mountain already has digital order capture. The missing layer is fast confidence, not another static catalog screen.

Current reality

Orders can be started online, but the most important answer still lives outside the screen: can this job be fulfilled from the right yard, on the right timing, with the right substitute if needed?

A
Catalog first Customer or sales rep builds the list using the online plant catalog.
B
Verification second Inventory still needs a human check because counts can move throughout the day.
C
Transfer logic third Nearby, statewide, and vendor paths are coordinated manually across teams.

Proposed mobile control

Give the internal team one mobile view that ranks fulfillment options, flags risk early, and accelerates substitute approval before the order becomes a service problem.

1
Best source picked instantly System shows the strongest yard, the next-best transfer lane, and confidence level in one screen.
2
Low-confidence lines escalated Only exceptions trigger manual checks, which cuts repeat calls and hidden ops work.
3
Substitutes approved with context Sales can respond faster because design fit, ETA, and operational cost are already visible.
