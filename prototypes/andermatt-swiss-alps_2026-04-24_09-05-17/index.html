Steady support for a growing Alpine destination

Your digital estate now touches owners, guests, and staff across one Prime Alpine Destination. Support has to stay calm when winter traffic, service requests, and vendor handovers all peak together.

We noticed Andermatt Swiss Alps is hiring a Head of IT-Support. That usually means the service model needs stronger process and tooling before more brands and seasonal demand stack up.

Our Read

The hiring signal says Andermatt is moving from helpful local support to multi-brand service operations. Your team already supports Hospitality, Real Estate, and Corporate units, while owner service and guest journeys stay digital. That puts pressure on ticket routing, vendor control, knowledge sharing, and project handover. When those seams stay loose, small issues turn into repeat contacts, slower fixes, and less trust from owners, guests, and staff.

What We'd Do

We would start with a scoped 8-week service operations project, delivered end to end by Elinext. The goal is to make support clearer, faster, and easier to run.

01

Map the real service load

Trace incidents and requests across Hospitality, Real Estate, Corporate, myASA, and owner services, then define clear owners, SLAs, escalations, and missing runbooks.

02

Clean up the support flow

Rebuild ticket categories, self-service paths, and knowledge content so common owner, guest, and workplace issues reach the right team fast.

03

Fix the noisy digital journeys

Start with complaint reporting, service booking, payment handoff, and mobile information findability where unclear steps create repeat contacts and extra support work.

04

Make handover ready for peak season

Put project-to-operations handover in place with vendor rules, weekly reporting, root-cause loops, and practical on-site readiness before winter demand climbs.
