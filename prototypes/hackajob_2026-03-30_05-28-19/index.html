What if Archer fixed the silence after the match?

Public feedback says the relevance is strong, but trust starts leaking once a candidate is matched and then waits for the next signal. This concept adds a mobile confidence layer for candidates and a rescue queue for recruiters without breaking hackajob’s ATS-first workflow.

Evidence

Why this concept is plausible

The prototype is anchored in public signals, not guesswork. The goal is to increase confidence and conversion without replacing the existing web platform or ATS integrations.

Strong product, thin follow-through Trustpilot and Reddit both point to the same issue: people like the idea and sometimes the outcomes, but they want more visible motion after interest is shown. Trustpilot + Reddit
Notifications are part of the value G2 feedback explicitly asks for more frequent updates when companies start searching or interacting, which makes a mobile layer a direct product response. G2 reviews
ATS remains the operating system Archer already routes into existing employer workflows. The right mobile product is an engagement shell, not a workflow rewrite. Official Archer + integrations pages
Executive Angle

Why this matters for Mark Chaffey

The company story is relevance, quality, and lower recruiter admin. A mobile confidence layer sharpens all three without making hackajob look like it is abandoning the ATS-first architecture that helps it sell.

Protect the brand promise If Archer says “you genuinely match,” the product should keep proving that after the match, not stop at the first notification.
Increase interview yield High-fit candidates are most valuable when their intent stays warm. Mobile nudges and visible response windows reduce passive drop-off.
Stay ATS compatible The concept adds a mobile engagement shell on top of current routing, source tracking, and recruiter workflows instead of replacing them.
Elinext Point Of View

Mobile is the missing trust layer

Archer already knows how to match. The next gain is showing candidates and recruiters exactly what happens next, on a device they actually react to in real time.
