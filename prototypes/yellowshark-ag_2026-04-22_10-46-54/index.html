Our read

The strain looks cross-system.

The hiring signal points to delivery pressure, not simple team growth. yellowshark now runs a job site, a worker app, and back-office flows. Those flows cover reports, documents, payroll help, and multilingual support. If releases stay split, candidates wait, staff chase fixes, and service quality drops. That is why infrastructure work matters here.

What we'd do

One small squad. One hard lane.

We would run one dedicated squad under your product owner and focus on the release path that touches candidates, workers, and recruiter teams most.

1

Map the release chain

Map website, app, documents, and CRM handoffs first. Remove unclear owners, manual checks, and slow release steps.

2

Stabilize the risky flows

Set one build and test lane for login, reports, documents, and payroll help. These flows carry the highest support cost.

3

Add guardrails and visibility

Add monitoring, access checks, and rollback rules for each release. Make production issues easy to see and fix.

4

Run a six-week pilot

Ship one pilot with weekly demos and clear release notes. Keep the lane only if it saves time fast.
