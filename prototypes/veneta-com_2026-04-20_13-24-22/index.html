Our Read

The signal says Veneta is treating customer contact as a growth system. That makes sense. Your model mixes webshop ordering, advice at home, online meethulp, and order tracking. Reviews stay strong overall. The misses repeat in familiar places. Missed appointments, wrong measurements, slow callbacks, and unclear delivery steps. If those handoffs stay manual, the service team absorbs the load. And the brand promise gets harder to keep.

What We’d Do

We would start with a dedicated squad under Veneta’s product and service owner. The goal is simple: fewer avoidable contacts, clearer customer steps, and faster recovery when exceptions happen.

Team Shape
1 tech lead, 2 web engineers, 1 designer, 1 QA
Pilot Window
8 weeks, weekly demos, shared backlog
Relevant Stack
React Node.js Python Java
1

Map the real service path

Map webshop, online meethulp, and advice-at-home handoffs. Remove duplicate steps, hidden ownership, and missing status signals before they become calls.

2

Fix the highest-cost moments

Build a clearer layer for appointments, callbacks, and order status. Show the next step earlier and route exception cases faster.

3

Turn repeat issues into rules

Add QA checks around measurement changes, wrong-color claims, missing parts, and delivery delays. Replace team memory with tested flows.

4

Support agents with AI help

Pilot an AI-assisted reply and triage helper for repeat questions. Keep agents in control, but cut manual lookup time.
