Proposal Brief

Make Charterhouse's tech hiring portal easier to run and trust.

A focused proposal for the web portal behind jobs, alerts, resumes, and candidate accounts. The aim is faster recruiter work, safer data flow, and a better first step for senior technology candidates.

Why we're here. We saw Charterhouse running senior searches such as Data & AI Governance Lead and security leadership roles. That usually means more sensitive data, tighter client updates, and more pressure on the portal that supports jobs, alerts, and applications.

What we see today

Public jobs Search, save, alerts, and resume flow live together.
Candidate accounts The login path is cleaner than the public homepage.
Senior tech mandates Cyber, governance, and delivery roles raise the trust bar.
Our Read

Our read

These searches show that Charterhouse is moving deeper into high-trust technology mandates. The public site already joins job search, saved jobs, alerts, resume intake, consultant pages, and account login on one stack. When that stack slows down or breaks, recruiters lose time and candidates lose confidence. The cost is slower shortlists, more manual follow-up, and weaker visibility for heads of technology and operations.

Eight-week pilot steps

01
Map the real handoffs

Map each step across jobs, account login, alerts, saved jobs, and resume intake to find drop-offs, duplicate fields, and slow manual handoffs.

02
Clean candidate flow

Tighten candidate account rules, secure forms, and audit trails so security, data, and infrastructure mandates move with cleaner records and less rework.

03
Add recruiter ops views

Add a recruiter ops layer for shortlist status, client-ready notes, and search health reporting across cyber, governance, and applications roles.

04
Ship with safer checks

Set up QA automation and release checks for the public site and logged-in flow before each job push or content update.
