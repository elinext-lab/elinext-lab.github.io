Proposal Brief for Gary Lai

Make Charterhouse's candidate flow fit complex tech searches

This brief focuses on the dashboard and job flow Charterhouse already owns. The aim is simple: cut candidate friction, give consultants cleaner signals, and support higher-value data, AI, and operations mandates.

We noticed Charterhouse is running data-governance and IT delivery searches. That usually means more complex briefs, more candidate states, and more delivery pressure moving through one shared workflow.

Signal

Data, AI, and IT delivery roles are now visible on the Charterhouse funnel.

Product Surface

Why it matters

When the brief is complex, small workflow gaps slow shortlists and weaken trust.

Our Read

Our read

The signal tells us Charterhouse is carrying more senior, technical briefs through a platform built for broad recruitment volume. Your site already spans search, saved jobs, alerts, LinkedIn login, and application states. That spread makes it hard to keep candidate context, trust, and consultant ownership clean. When the brief is data governance or IT delivery, small workflow gaps turn into slower shortlists, repeat outreach, and weaker reporting.

Pilot milestones

1

Map the live workflow

Map the real path from search jobs to save, alert, login, and apply, then mark the points where consultant context drops or duplicates.

2

Unify candidate state

Unify candidate state data so saved jobs, alerts, applications, and recruiter follow-up share one clear record for tech, data, and supply chain briefs.

3

Fix the risky screens

Tighten the highest-risk screens first: role context, identity cues, status messages, and handoff points around LinkedIn sign-in and application submission.

4

Ship and measure weekly

Ship weekly improvements with QA, event tracking, and a simple ops dashboard so your team can see response, completion, and stuck states fast.
