Why This Matters

The public sources all point to the same operational risk: AD Mortgage is building a modern borrower app on top of a servicing environment that can still change owners, channels, and support queues underneath the user.

03/09

Fresh launch, no store proof yet

The iOS app released on March 9, 2026 and still shows zero US ratings. Android is at `50+` installs with no visible rating activity.

3x

Servicer sprawl is public

AD Mortgage's own `My Mortgage` page points borrowers to eStatus Connect, YourMortgageOnline, and Shellpoint depending on servicing ownership.

4

Support queues leak into UX

Servicing, insurance/tax, loss mitigation, and payoff requests each use separate support channels on the public site.

What Elinext Would Build

The fastest win is not a full-platform rewrite. It is a mobile trust layer that sits across the existing servicing stack and gives borrowers one answer to the question: “what happens next, and who owns it?”

Transfer-safe servicing

Borrower clarity
  • Keep one payment and document history even after servicing moves.
  • Show the active servicer without sending the borrower into portal roulette.
  • Surface transfer checklists before confusion turns into support calls.

Issue routing with ownership

Ops visibility
  • Classify servicing, insurance, tax, payoff, and hardship issues from one inbox.
  • Attach vendor / team ownership and SLA to every open case.
  • Preserve conversation context across AI and human handoffs.

Launch-stage trust layer

Brand confidence
  • Expose real-time payment status and next-step confirmations.
  • Make document sync, insurance alerts, and payment routing explicit.
  • Give new app users a smoother first-run experience while public trust signals are still thin.

For Lana / COO

One borrower surface with traceable ownership reduces call-center noise and makes servicing handoffs measurable instead of anecdotal.

For vendor management

A case-level audit trail lets AD see where a partner, servicer, or internal queue is creating delay or confusion.

For mobile growth

The app moves from “another place to log in” to the durable source of truth for payment, support, and transfer confidence.
