What Reviews Actually Say

  • Crashes and freezing

    Technicians repeatedly describe the app freezing during note entry, dropdown use, or ordinary navigation.

  • Photo uploads take minutes

    Multiple recent reviews mention 9 to 10 minutes to upload a small photo set or outright media failure.

  • Data loss changed user behavior

    At least one Android reviewer writes reports in Gmail first because they do not trust the app to keep the draft.

Why This Matters To BuildOps

  • The mobile app is the product in the field

    When the technician experience breaks, platform trust and retention break with it.

  • AI value is being undercut

    Reviewers explicitly push back on AI features when basics like notes and uploads still feel unreliable.

  • This is a CTO-level issue

    Duncan Grazier was hired to drive next-gen innovation. Reliability is the fastest route to visible product leverage.

Suggested Delivery Sprint

  • Week 1: instrumentation

    Trace crashes, stalled sync, media latency, and background resume failure.

  • Week 2: draft-safe core

    Persist notes, partial forms, and closeout state locally by default.

  • Week 3: queue and compression

    Background uploads, retry visibility, and fewer taps on top technician actions.

Three Product Changes Worth Shipping First

These are practical mobile improvements tied directly to review language, not speculative redesign theater.

1. Offline-safe notes and closeout drafts

Assume interruption is normal. Every keystroke and checklist state should survive app switches, camera launch, and low-signal pockets.

2. Background media pipeline

Photos should enter a visible queue instantly, upload when possible, and never block technician progress on the current visit.

3. Context-first job history

Let technicians reach prior asset visits, photos, and last-service notes in one tap instead of scanning layers of screens.
