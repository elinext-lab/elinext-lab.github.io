Proposal for Martin Mayer

A focused STELLA sprint for cleaner recruiting and faster growth

STELLA already carries a broad promise: multi-posting, analytics, ATS handoff, and regional hiring scale. The next step is not a redesign. It is making that promise easier to deliver and easier to trust.

We saw a Head of IT search in your hiring mix. That usually means software delivery and recruiting operations are rising together. This page is about keeping STELLA reliable while that pressure grows.

Our read

The signal tells us Iventa is balancing service work, client hiring demand, and product scale at once. Public pages show three linked products: STELLA, Smart Transfer, and JobPost API. Each one depends on clean ATS and media handoffs. TOWA also notes that the platform had to connect with two internal systems. When those links slow down, consultants spend time fixing flow gaps. That means slower sales, heavier support, and a weaker demo story.

What we'd do

We would start with a six-week scoped project. The goal is simple: remove friction from posting, transfer, and reporting before it turns into a bigger roadmap tax.

Engagement model
Scoped end-to-end project
Team shape
1 tech lead, 2 web engineers, 1 QA, 1 designer
Cadence
Weekly demos, shared backlog, handover in week 6
Relevant stack
React, Node.js, .NET, Angular, QA automation
01

Map the real handoffs

Trace STELLA, Smart Transfer, and JobPost API across ATS, billing, and reporting. Flag the joins, retries, and manual checks that cost the most time.

02

Harden core posting flow

Stabilize the route from job order to live media channels. Add better error states, retries, logs, and release checks for DACH-critical paths.

03

Cut admin waste

Trim duplicate entry around packages, applicant transfer, and campaign edits. Make the client and consultant path shorter, clearer, and easier to support.

04

Leave a usable system

Ship fixes in small batches, write practical test coverage, and hand back a ranked backlog. Your team keeps the gains after the sprint.
