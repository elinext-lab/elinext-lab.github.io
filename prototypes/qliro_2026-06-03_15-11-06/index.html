Qliro Checkout needs extra delivery capacity without slowing trust

A short engineering note on checkout capacity, payment-flow reliability, and a first squad plan for Qliro Checkout Gen 3, with research behind each next step.

Why we're here: saw Qliro hiring a Software Engineer - Checkout while also listing wider product roles. That points to a live push on merchant checkout, not a back-office project.

Why we're here Checkout Gen 3 is a growth path. It also widens the surface for regressions, plugins, and payment status edge cases.

Our read

Qliro is moving from a payment provider to a merchant growth engine. The Software Engineer - Checkout role says the pressure is in the full checkout loop: React UI, .NET services, AWS, observability, and release quality. Qliro Checkout Gen 3 adds B2B, in-store, loyalty sign-up, shipping, and Unified Payments into one path. That is a lot to ship while merchants expect safe, simple payments every day. If capacity stays tight, feature work and hardening fight for the same engineers.

Where we'd start

Noticed

Recent Android and iOS reviews repeat payment blockers: frozen screens, blank invoice views, weak reminders, and VoiceOver labels that say only "button." Start with an accessibility and payment-completion sweep tied to BankID, invoice, reminder, and return flows.

Next step

A WooCommerce merchant thread describes duplicate callbacks around Qliro for WooCommerce and TWO Invoice. Add idempotency tests around transaction IDs, webhooks, and retry paths before more partner flows go live.
