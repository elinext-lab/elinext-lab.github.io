You are building a Prime Alpine Destination. That promise needs calm digital service across stays, apartments, dining, and support.

Our read

Guest service now spans several systems

The hiring signal says support is now a growth issue, not a side task. Guests move between the stay site, Alpine Apartments, and the service desk. Teams then inherit issues across booking, check-in, content, and account flows. When ownership stays split, small faults take longer to close. In a premium destination, that weakens the guest experience James Lamb is expected to protect.

01

Map the service joins

Review the stay site, Alpine Apartments, Freshdesk, and partner flows. Build one support map with named owners and one list of repeat guest issues.

02

Fix the repeat triggers

Fix the top friction points first: login, booking handoff, check-in messages, and document access. Add one clear path for vendor escalation.

03

Set one release rhythm

Set one weekly release rhythm for guest-facing systems. Use shared QA checks, rollback notes, and short status updates for hospitality and IT.

04

See issues sooner

Add simple monitoring around booking, content, and support entry points. The team should see failures before guests, staff, or partners report them.
