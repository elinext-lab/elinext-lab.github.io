Our read

The signal points to a handoff problem

The role tells us Ketjen is tightening plant standards and automation work. Public pages say your team also runs secure data exchange, unit monitoring, and customer dashboards. That mix often creates slow handoffs between site engineers, analysts, and portal teams. If the flow stays split, customers wait longer, engineers recheck data, and change becomes risky.

What we’d do

A small delivery squad around the data-to-portal path

We would run a dedicated squad under your PO or PM. The goal is one calmer path from plant data to portal output.

01

Trace the live flow

Map plant, lab, and portal handoffs first. Cut the risky re-entry points around Amsterdam engineering, E-cat analysis, and customer follow-up.

02

Build one integration layer

Connect source systems, user access, and result delivery through one audited service layer. Remove duplicate data entry and reduce trust gaps.

03

Strengthen the portal workflow

Ship cleaner result views, alert rules, and support actions for the E-cat path. Keep role-based access and release safety in scope.

04

Keep change safe

Stand up QA gates, monitoring, and weekly demos. Your team keeps control while product changes move faster and with less risk.
