We saw the Data Transformation Director mandate for a PE-backed engineering business. The role is not just search; it asks for execution across legacy data, AI-ready architecture, Azure, and Fabric.

Our read

The public Data Transformation Director brief shows a client moving from assessment into delivery. It names a legacy-to-modern data platform, AI-native architecture, Azure, and Microsoft Fabric. That means the risk is not finding a smart leader only. The harder gap is giving that leader builders who can ship pipelines, models, tests, and handover notes. If delivery starts late, the placed leader may spend the first month rebuilding capacity instead of moving the platform.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

The mandate says "pure execution" after strategy. Build a two-week starter pack: source audit, migration risks, first Fabric model, and delivery backlog.
