Our read

The signal says Monument is adding savings product capacity while Monument Technology turns bank rails into a BPaaS offer. That means the same teams may need to protect the app, savings journeys, and platform integrations at once. The app is strong, but users still surface sharp edge cases around ISA transfers, one-device login, and linked-account payments. Those moments matter because Monument serves clients moving large balances. If they feel stuck, the promise of simple, human banking gets harder to keep.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

Device switching protects trust, but it can feel rough.

The one-device rule is clear in the FAQ, and one iOS reviewer called the switch flow annoying. We would test warning copy, lost-phone recovery, and approval states before more users hit it.

Useful next step

ISA transfer status needs stronger guardrails.

Reviews and Reddit mention transfer provider gaps, slow release, and unclear follow-up. Map each transfer state, add proactive status copy, and route stuck cases before the queue grows.
