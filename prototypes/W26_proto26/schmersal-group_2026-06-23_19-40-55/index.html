Prepared for Erwin Neelen

Help new logistics hires move Schmersal data faster

Erwin, we saw Schmersal growing Materialwirtschaft und Logistik while your applications team supports SAP, webshop, and safety-product data. This brief shows where we would lower friction first.

Why we're here: when a manufacturer adds logistics capacity, the application layer must keep pace. The first risk is not headcount. It is slow handoff between SAP, catalogue, warehouse, and reporting work.

Moment we noticed Materials and logistics growth, with IT Applications close to SAP and product-data flows.

Our read

The logistics role tells us Schmersal is scaling material flow, not only hiring another operator. That usually puts pressure on SAP, stock visibility, product data, labels, and exception handling. Your new webshop already ties search, product information, and ordering into one customer path. SLC Assist also sends product selection back into the online catalogue. If those data paths are not clean inside, new logistics staff may spend time checking systems instead of moving material.

Where we'd start

Two small checks can reduce risk before bigger work starts.

Webshop data

Your online catalogue joins product search, product information, and ordering. First, map who owns material master, stock status, documents, and order state across SAP and the webshop.

Field to stock

SLC Assist stores device status and points users to catalogue products. Next, test common product and asset IDs from field documentation into warehouse and service flows.

What we'd do

A focused pilot around one material flow, not a broad program.

We would run a dedicated squad under your product owner or application lead. The team shape: one tech lead, two backend engineers, one QA automation engineer, and one data engineer.
