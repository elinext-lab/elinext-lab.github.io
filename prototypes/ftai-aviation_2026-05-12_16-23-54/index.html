Proposal brief for FTAI Aviation

Lower support load across FTAI's engine shop visits

FTAI is growing around CFM56 and V2500 engines, module exchange, and customer transparency. Software should help supply chain teams see status, parts, scope, and customer questions faster.

We saw the Customer Support Engineer role in Montreal. It asks one person to track shop events, answer technical questions, and keep customers and MRO teams aligned.

Why this matters now Support demand is close to live engine events, not only help desk tickets.

Our read

The role says support demand is moving closer to live shop work. Engine events now need clear status, deviation, and inventory data across FTAI teams, MRO sites, and airline customers. If those updates live in email, Excel, or separate portals, Michael's team must chase facts instead of managing flow. That slows decisions during tight shop visits and creates more customer questions.

What we'd do

Use a dedicated Elinext squad under your product or operations lead. Start with a six-week pilot focused on one shop-visit support flow and the data around it.
