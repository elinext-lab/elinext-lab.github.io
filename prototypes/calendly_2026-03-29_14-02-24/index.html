The real gap is not booking. It is recovery.

Calendly already owns the simple part: link sharing and fast scheduling. The failure point is the messy part after that: stale one-time links, sync drift, opaque "slot filled" errors, and mobile users who still cannot fix exceptions in place.

Why this direction fits Calendly right now

The company signal is platform scale. The user signal is fragile exception handling. That makes this a strong fit for a targeted mobile systems feature instead of a cosmetic redesign.

Reduce support pull

Broken bookings become self-serve recoveries. The prototype replaces generic error states and help-article deflection with clear host actions.

Protect booking trust

Calendar drift, reused links, and host overrides are where confidence drops. Resolving those moments on mobile keeps the product promise intact.

Turn friction into telemetry

A logged rescue flow gives platform and product teams structured evidence about recurring sync failures, stale links, and account-state mismatches.
