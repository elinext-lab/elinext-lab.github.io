A clearer payroll flow for Quantum’s worker and branch teams

A short plan to make Quantum’s payroll flow simpler, safer, and easier to support across timesheets, supervisor approvals, and account access for every assignment.

We saw Quantum is hiring a payroll lead. That often means timesheets, approvals, and pay runs need tighter control right now.

Why we are here

  • Primary productQuantum Mobile plus the my account portal

Our Read

What we think is happening

Your site promises turnkey payrolling and value-add staffing services. Hiring a payroll lead suggests the load is growing across branches. The worker journey also spans a legacy account portal and the Quantum Mobile timesheet app. When that flow breaks, staff answer support calls, approvals slow down, and payroll trust drops.

What We’d Do

A six-week payroll flow pilot

Engagement model

We would start with a scoped end-to-end project. One team owns discovery, delivery, and handoff.

Team shape

1 tech lead, 2 senior .NET engineers, 1 mobile engineer, 1 QA.

Duration

Six weeks, with a live pilot in one branch first.

Relevant stack
.NET C# React Node.js Native Android
01

Map the payroll path

Map every worker, supervisor, and branch action from login to approved pay. Fix the fields, rules, and edge cases that create manual follow-up.

02

Stabilize the account layer

Stabilize the account and timesheet layer with better validation, clearer status messages, and safer error handling in the old .NET flow.

03

Repair the mobile flow

Refresh the mobile path so assignment workers can submit hours fast, see pay status, and recover access without calling a branch.

04

Ship with weekly proof

Set weekly demos, release checks, and QA gates so payroll, support, and operations see progress early and ship with less risk.
