Proposal Brief for SSTL

Support SSTL's mission build work without pulling engineers off spacecraft

You already have the mission teams. The gap is often the software around them: data flow, test evidence, sign-off packs, and clear status across build, integration, and operations.

We saw SSTL is hiring AIT, harness, and thermal contractors right now. That usually means several programmes are moving together and internal tools need to keep up.

Why We're Here

  • AIT contractor hiring
  • Harness work in flight build
  • Thermal workload rising

That is a strong signal of delivery pressure around the mission life-cycle, not just normal hiring.

Our Read

Those hires tell us you are carrying several mission workstreams at once. When assembly, integration, test, harness, and thermal teams are stretched, the first thing to slow down is the software around them. Test evidence, action lists, sign-off packs, and readiness views end up split across files, inboxes, and separate systems. That steals senior engineer time and makes reviews harder than they should be. The cost is not only delay. It is focus lost from the core spacecraft work.

What We'd Do

We would run a dedicated software squad under your engineering lead for one quarter, focused on one job: cut the admin and reporting load around spacecraft build and test.

01

Map the live handoffs

Map the current AIT, harness, thermal, and quality handoffs. Lock one owner and one source of truth for each mission step.

02

Build one shared workspace

Build a secure workspace for test evidence, action logs, NCRs, harness revisions, and sign-offs, so teams stop chasing the latest PDF by email.

03

Connect the systems already in use

Connect SAP, shared files, and calculator or lab outputs into simple status views that show blockers, overdue actions, and review readiness by mission.

04

Leave a tool your team can run

Ship QA-backed workflows, train the internal owners, and leave clear handover notes so the tool keeps working when the pilot ends.

Engagement Dedicated squad under your engineering lead, with weekly demos and a 10-week pilot scope.
Team Shape 1 tech lead, 2 senior .NET and React engineers, 1 data and integration engineer, 1 QA.
Relevant Stack C#, .NET, React, Python, and Node.js.
