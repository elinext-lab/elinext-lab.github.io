Stabilizing myGwork as platform growth gets harder to manage

myGwork is now more than a community site. It is a live talent platform with jobs, networking, mentoring, events, Academy, and employer services. That makes reliability part of your brand.

We saw signs that myGwork is putting more weight on IT delivery and transformation. That fits a platform that just upgraded key flows. It now carries more demand, more ATS links, and more event traffic.

Why this moment matters

  • Job applications leave myGwork and jump into partner ATS flows.
  • Public reviews keep pointing at broken apply, sign-in, and mobile paths.
  • WorkPride, WorkFair, and employer campaigns raise the cost of every weak release.
Our Read

Where the pressure seems to be

myGwork looks like a platform at an inflection point. The product promise is broad. Jobs, networking, events, Academy, and employer services now share one surface. That creates fragile handoffs between member journeys, partner job feeds, and live event peaks. If those weak points stay, support load rises, applications drop, and employer trust gets harder to keep.

What We’d Do

A short hardening pilot around the flows that carry trust

01

Map the real apply path

Map the full apply path across myGwork, partner ATS pages, and app handoffs. We log where users drop, fail, or loop back to support.

02

Fix the highest-friction journeys

03

Reduce release risk around WorkPride, WorkFair, and partner campaigns. We add regression suites, roll-out controls, and clear incident ownership.

04

Tighten employer-side proof

Tighten partner integrations and employer reporting. We make ATS feeds, job sync, and event sign-up data easier to trust and support.
