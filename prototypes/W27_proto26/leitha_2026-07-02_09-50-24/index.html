Scaling Moty's backend without slowing city mobility pilots

Saw the Backend Engineer role for Leithà's Computer Science team. It points to more API, microservice, and cloud work around data-intensive products like Moty.

Our read

The Backend Engineer signal says Leithà is scaling the engine under its data products. Moty shows the shape of that work: background trip capture, phone login, weekly travel stats, and aggregate insights for cities and research partners. The hard part is not one feature. It is clean APIs, safe queues, useful data contracts, and fast fixes when mobile data is late or incomplete. If that stays inside a small core team, pilots slow down and senior engineers get pulled from product decisions. A focused outside backend squad can take that load without changing ownership.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

Moty says users must grant permissions and answer trip questions to keep weekly stats useful. First step: add an event audit for permission drop-off, unsynced trips, and incomplete trip answers.

Useful next step

Moty Play ended, but its reward logic touched login, trips, tokens, and draws. Keep future campaigns behind a small service boundary, away from core trip ingestion.
