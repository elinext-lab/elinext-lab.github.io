Our read

The internal-tooling signal says Tandem may be tightening the work behind billing, invoicing, and daily operations. At the same time, your public patient path crosses Athenahealth for the portal, Luma for online scheduling, and separate pharmacy refill links. That split can help you move fast, but it also creates handoffs that staff must explain and support. If internal tools lag behind those patient steps, billing questions and refill follow-up can pull people back to manual work. The risk is not one broken app; it is small gaps across care access, pharmacy, and back office work.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

Map every patient action from the homepage: appointment, portal account, refill, bill pay, and pharmacy links.

Useful next step

Tag the handoff owner and system of record for each step, so staff can see where a patient is stuck.

What reviews suggest

Athena app users often mention message alerts, login friction, appointment details, and prescription routing.

Useful next step

Turn those themes into QA checks for Tandem's portal support scripts and linked patient paths.

What we'd do

Run a six-week dedicated squad under Tandem's owner. The goal is not a new portal. It is better workflow visibility across the tools patients already touch.
