Nexuzhealth is growing a connected EHR platform across hospitals, labs, caregivers, and patients. This page shows where a small Java-led squad could reduce delivery risk fast.

Our read

Gijs, the Java role says the core platform is moving while product demand keeps rising. Nexuzhealth now links CWS, Mynexuzhealth, Consult, Nurse, and Lab around one central EHR. That creates delivery pressure at the same point users feel login, sync, and record-flow issues. Android reviews mention itsme login failures, initial data sync stalls, and appointment buttons hidden by Android controls. If modernization slips, new modules slow down while trust in the patient portal takes the hit.

Where we'd start

Noticed

Recent Android reviews cluster around itsme login, sync stalls, and settings crashes. Build a release-gate test pack for authentication browsers, Shibboleth redirects, and first sync.

Next step

The FAQ splits exclusions between eHealth and Nexuzhealth paths. Map that journey and add support prompts where patients must leave Mynexuzhealth.

What we'd do

Use a dedicated squad under your product owner and architecture lead. Team shape: one Java tech lead, two senior Java engineers, one QA automation engineer, and one DevOps engineer.
