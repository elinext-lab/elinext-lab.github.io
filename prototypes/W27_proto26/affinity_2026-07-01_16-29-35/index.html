Affinity can scale Java delivery without slowing Keywork

Keywork brings recruitment, staffing, analytics, and employee self-service into one HR platform. The next question is how fast the team can add capacity without losing quality.

Why we're here: Affinity is hiring Java, full-stack, Golang, OutSystems, and platform roles. That points to live delivery load across client work and your own HR product surface.

Our read

The hiring mix says Affinity is scaling more than one team at once. Java and platform roles suggest backend load, while full-stack roles point to product and client delivery pressure. Keywork makes this sharper, because it covers recruitment, staffing, analytics, and an employee portal in one product. That breadth can slow releases if senior engineers must review every flow. If capacity is added too late, work moves, but quality standards and sprint focus can suffer.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

On Keywork mobile, the audit flagged the hero app image and lower-page backgrounds as too large for their displayed size. First step: add responsive images and WebP/AVIF before the page grows.

Useful next step

The same audit found low-contrast grey text in the edition cards. First step: fix the copy tokens and add contrast checks to the release pipeline.

What we'd do

Use a dedicated squad under Affinity's PO or PM for a six-week pilot, with weekly demos and clear code ownership.
