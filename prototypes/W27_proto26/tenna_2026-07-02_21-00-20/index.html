Our read

The hiring signal says Tenna is scaling the platform behind mixed-fleet operations, not just adding one feature. Your public role asks for Node.js, AWS, Docker, RabbitMQ, Kinesis, large data systems, and microservices. That points to pressure around ingesting field events, inspections, telematics, and work orders without slowing crews down. Android reviews mention inspections that would not load, crashes, logouts, BLE battery drain, and scan failures. If those edges stay noisy while Deere grows Tenna, field trust gets harder to protect.

Where we'd start

Field sync path

First, trace the inspection and scan paths from mobile offline queue to backend service ack. Reviewers call out failed vehicle inspections and camera scan trouble, so a small reliability map would show whether the break is client state, sync, or event processing.

Android release checks

Second, use release notes and store reviews to compare Android and iOS field workflows. Battery-heavy BLE scanning needs a clear on/off policy and a background-job test suite.
