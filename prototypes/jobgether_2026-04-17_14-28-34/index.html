Proposal brief for Jobgether

Make Jobgether’s candidate journey feel as strong as its matching.

This brief focuses on the service layer around Jobgether’s web platform. The goal is to reduce trust loss in key support and status moments.

You are hiring a CX Services Program Manager. That usually means support, product, and ops are all carrying more load. We built this page around that moment.

Why this feels timely

  • Public complaints point to stale job states and support churn.
  • Premium, auto-apply, and employer tools now share one service path.
  • A short sprint can tighten trust without a full rebuild.

Our read

The signal says Jobgether is moving from fast product growth to repeatable service design. The platform now spans match score, auto-apply, premium coaching, employer tools, and billing. When those flows drift, users do not blame the source. They blame Jobgether. Public complaints about stale roles, sign-up friction, and payment confusion show where CX work quickly becomes trust work.

What we’d do

We would start with a scoped 6-week CX reliability sprint, reviewed each week with your product owner.

Six weeks. Four focused moves.

This is a practical proposal, not a rewrite. Each step targets the moments where status gaps create distrust.

01

Journey map and signal audit

Map the live path from LinkedIn click to support resolution, including stale-job handling, premium upgrade, refund flow, and close-state sync.

02

Fix the high-friction screens

Repair expired-role messages, improve premium copy, and clean up search controls so mobile actions feel clearer and more accessible.

03

Give CX ops better visibility

Add case routing, status audit trails, and alerts when a role closes upstream but still appears in user-facing flows.

04

Ship with QA discipline

Run weekly demos, QA automation, and voice-of-customer review so new CX fixes land safely and stay measurable.
