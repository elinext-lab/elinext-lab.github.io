The hard part is not traffic. It is flow quality.

Your public site already runs job search, candidate sign-in, CV drop-off, and specialism pages across many markets. The Service Architect signal suggests these flows now depend on more services, rules, and integrations than one team can keep tidy. When that layer gets messy, candidate quality drops, consultants spend time on manual fixes, and client enquiries move slower than they should.

Map the full candidate path

02

Define cleaner service rules

Remove manual consultant work

Fix the highest-friction CRM and candidate-workflow gaps first, then add logs and alerts so recruitment consultants can trust every application handoff.

04

Add smoke tests, regression checks, and release notes around the candidate flows that matter most, with weekly demos and visible progress every sprint.
