Our read

The next bottleneck is not product vision

PraxisEins is moving from startup speed to network discipline. Public sources show booking, video visits, patient communication, and 24/7 support. The hiring signal says these flows now need one stronger IT core. If that base stays uneven, rollouts slow and local fixes spread.

What we'd do

A short project to build the operating base

Scoped 8-week foundation project

One team. One owner. Weekly demos.

We would run this as a scoped project. The goal is a safer IT base.

1
Map critical systems

Review booking, video visits, Microsoft 365, Telematik, and local setup. Mark what breaks often, who owns it, and where patient risk sits.

2
Set one security baseline

Standardize device, identity, backup, and incident rules for each practice. Close weak spots around shared accounts, vendor access, and endpoints.

3
Fix support flow

Create one ticket path, clear severity rules, and a weekly release window. Stop ad hoc fixes from blocking doctors, MFA teams, and operations.

4
Land two visible wins

Ship two small but real fixes during the pilot. Good targets are booking handoff, telemedicine support, or site IT reporting.

Team shape

1 delivery lead, 1 architect, 1 full-stack engineer, 1 DevOps and SecOps engineer, and 1 QA.

Working rhythm

Week 1 is discovery. Weeks 2 to 7 cover rollout and fixes. Week 8 is hardening and handover.
