Our Read

MPET's challenge is now service delivery discipline, not digital ambition.

Scope is growing faster than governance

The open Digital Service Delivery Manager role is the clearest public signal. MPET is explicitly looking for more structure around SLAs, stakeholder coordination, incident flow, and release rhythm.

Visibility still depends on trust in freshness

MPET Connect promises accurate and transparent data, but the FAQ still frames key visibility with scheduled refresh windows. That means delivery quality and user trust move together.

Truck appointments became a core lane fast

Truck appointments went live in late 2025, then became mandatory through MPET Connect shortly after. The 2026 release stream shows active tuning around reschedules, reporting, API visibility, and exceptions.

Every change touches a wide partner network

Shipping lines, inland terminals, barge, truck, rail, and warehouse operators all depend on the same service layer. That raises the cost of unclear ownership, fragmented support, and uneven rollout quality.

Public signals behind this read: MPET jobs, MPET Connect FAQ, MPET Connect release notes, and truck operator support.
What We'd Do

Build a delivery lane around MPET Connect that is easier to run, measure, and scale.

01

Map the live service estate

We would map the real operating lane across truck appointments, web visibility, API consumers, support content, and release dependencies. The goal is one agreed view of services, owners, SLAs, and failure points.

02

Create one service command view

We would connect the work backlog, incidents, release notes, and operational metrics into one delivery dashboard so MPET can see what changed, what broke, what is at risk, and who owns the response.

03

Harden the journeys that drive support load

We would focus on the flows most likely to create operator friction: booking changes, status freshness, exception handling, API clarity, and support handoff between partner-facing and internal teams.

04

Run a short pilot with clear exit targets

We would propose a six-week pilot with weekly demos, visible QA gates, and concrete goals such as lower incident noise, cleaner release readiness, and better transparency across owners.

Lean squad shape: 1 service delivery lead, 2 senior web/API engineers, 1 QA automation engineer, and 1 BA working with MPET product and service owners. This is small enough to move fast and structured enough to leave usable process behind.
