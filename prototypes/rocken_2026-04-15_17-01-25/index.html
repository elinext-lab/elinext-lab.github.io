Strengthening the system behind Rocken Jobs and Rocken Talent

We saw Rocken is hiring cloud, IT system, and Microsoft 365 roles. That usually means the platform, admin flow, and recruiter tooling all need more strength at once.

Rocken already promises smart matching, full transparency, and a simple process. The next step is making sure the systems behind that promise stay fast, clean, and in sync.

Public product Rocken runs a job platform and a talent profile layer, not just a classic agency site.
Public pressure Recent feedback points to stale roles, auto replies, and weak status clarity when the process slips.

Our Read

Those open roles tell us Rocken is tightening platform uptime and internal service speed together. Your sites promise a digital career profile, smart matching, and clear control for candidates. Public feedback suggests that promise can break when jobs stay visible too long, replies feel automatic, or status is hard to read. If cloud, admin, and Microsoft 365 handoffs stay manual, trust drops for both talent and partner companies.

What We’d Do

We would run a dedicated squad under your Product Owner for an eight-week pilot. The goal is simple: make the public promise and the internal process line up every day.

01

Map the real handoffs

Trace every step between Rocken Jobs, Talent, recruiter admins, and Microsoft 365. Mark where vacancy data, ownership, or status can drift.

02

Fix status sync first

Add event-based checks so closed jobs, profile states, and recruiter actions stay aligned across public pages, internal tools, and inbox workflows.

03

Harden the core funnel

Strengthen cloud hosting, monitoring, backups, and release steps around profile creation, document upload, matching, and recruiter follow-up paths.

04

Ship useful M365 automation

Build small recruiter tools for interview updates, reminder queues, and SLA views, then review impact with weekly demos and a clear backlog.
