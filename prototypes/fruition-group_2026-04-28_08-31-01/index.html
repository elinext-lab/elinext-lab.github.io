Elinext proposal brief

A delivery squad for Fruition's cloud and job platform work

Fruition is growing across technology, cloud, and project delivery. This brief shows where a small Elinext squad could reduce delivery load without changing your service model.

We noticed the Delivery Manager signal and the live Leeds role. It points to more delivery work around Job Search, CV drop-off, contractor onboarding, and reporting.

Our read

The signal says Fruition is tightening delivery while cloud and project work grow. The web platform now links job search, CV capture, ATS data, contractor services, and client reporting. That mix can create handoffs between recruiters, delivery leads, and systems. If those handoffs stay manual, strong candidate service becomes harder to scale.

What we'd do

Use a dedicated squad under Fruition's PO or PM for a six-week pilot, with a weekly demo and clear release notes.

  • 1 technical lead
  • 2 full-stack engineers
  • 1 QA automation engineer
  • 1 DevOps engineer
1

Map delivery handoffs

Map the Job Search, CV upload, Eploy, and recruiter handoffs. Mark every unclear state, duplicate field, and manual retry.

2

Fix candidate paths

Fix the public candidate path first. Improve filters, upload errors, labels, contrast, and crawlable job links.

3

Harden data flows

Harden ATS and contractor data flows. Add logging, retry rules, and checks for onboarding and timesheet paths.

4

Ship with proof

Ship weekly demos with QA evidence. Add smoke tests for search, forms, regional pages, and release notes.
