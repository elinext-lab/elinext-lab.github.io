Prototype Focus

Turn shortlist review into a single mobile motion.

The problem is not candidate discovery. The problem is what happens after the shortlist exists: feedback requests, approval chasing, and interview booking likely leak into inboxes and browser tabs. This concept keeps everything inside one narrow mobile surface with explainable AI and visible ownership.

Explainable ranking, not just AI branding

Each executive gets fit reasons, confidence, readiness, and explicit risk notes so founders and investors can approve faster without asking for a desktop deck.

Feedback stops living in scattered threads

Approvals, neutral votes, disapprovals, and comments sit on the same mobile card instead of drifting into email, WhatsApp, or Slack.

Book the interview inside the decision flow

Once enough approvals land, the next action is immediate: route to recruiter owner, show SLA, and confirm a meeting without context switching.

Grounded In Public Product Evidence

The idea stays close to what EVONA already ships.

We did not invent a new business model here. The concept is anchored in EVONA's public subscription engine, FRACTION's existing shortlist-and-feedback promise, and the real product visuals already on the site.

Current stack already supports product work

Public signals show a WordPress marketing layer, a React app shell, GTM, and CRM tooling. That is enough maturity for a focused mobile extension without a full platform reset.

The workflow is already valuable

Shortlists, feedback requests, messaging, and interview scheduling are the right primitives. The missing leverage is speed and visibility for stakeholders away from desktop.

The pitch matches Theo's remit

This is an AI and automation conversation first. Mobile is simply the fastest way to surface approvals, confidence, and next actions where busy decision-makers already are.

EVONA does not need another generic app.

It needs a thinner, faster mobile surface for the part of the workflow that actually decides whether a great executive gets booked or goes cold. That is the wedge this prototype is designed to show.
