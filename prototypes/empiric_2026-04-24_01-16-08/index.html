Proposal Brief for Sam Kamyar

A stronger application setup for Empiric's growing delivery work

Empiric already runs a global community across data, digital, cloud, security, and transformation hiring. As that reach grows, the candidate journey needs steadier rules. The apps behind it need cleaner handoffs and better visibility.

We saw Empiric is hiring an Operational Service Delivery Manager for applications. That usually means the public job board, recruiter tools, and back-office systems are under strain. One process owner can no longer carry the full load.

Signal

Operational service delivery ownership for applications.

Product

Empiric Job Board and candidate-facing web flow.

Focus

Candidate handoffs, status control, and app support visibility.

Our Read

Our read

That hiring signal points to scale. Empiric now serves many countries, practices, and client needs from one brand. The jobs page already carries a large role list and browser-side filters. That is fine early on. It gets harder when hiring, consent, support, and reporting all depend on separate application steps. Then delivery slows, candidate trust drops, and teams spend time chasing status.

What We'd Do

What we'd do

We would start with a dedicated squad under your delivery or product owner. A six-week pilot is enough to map the flow. It also removes the riskiest gaps and leaves a clearer service model behind.

Engagement Model

Dedicated squad under your owner, with weekly demos and one clear work backlog.

Team Shape

1 tech lead, 2 senior web engineers, 1 business analyst, 1 QA engineer.

Relevant Stack
Angular React .NET C# Node.js
01 Map the full flow

Map every handoff across the job board, recruiter workflow, CV sharing, application support, and reporting so one delivery owner can see where work stalls.

02 Tighten status control

Stabilize the candidate and consultant flow with clearer statuses, audit points, and rule-based routing for jobs, submissions, and follow-up tasks.

03 Build one operations view

Build an operations view for service delivery with backlog, SLA risk, stuck applications, and cross-country ownership in one place.

04 Make releases safer

Set a release rhythm, test coverage, and support playbook so changes to core applications stop creating manual rework for recruiters and managers.
