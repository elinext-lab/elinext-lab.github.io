Our Read

What the signal means

The signal tells us Manpower Switzerland is dealing with more supplier-led staffing work, not only more vacancy volume. TalentSight promises end-to-end visibility, but more suppliers, workers, and reports create debt fast. Public app feedback around search, account updates, and slow screens suggests weak handoffs across the wider digital estate. If that stays unresolved, recruiters spend time fixing states by hand and clients lose trust in the portal.

What We’d Do

A six-week platform hardening pilot

We would start with a dedicated squad under your product or operations lead for a six-week pilot. The goal is to cut handoff loss across TalentSight, worker admin, and candidate flows.

01

Map the real workflow

Trace TalentSight, WebTime, and candidate handoffs across requisitions, supplier actions, contracts, time reports, and client-facing status views that still depend on manual checks.

02

Fix the highest-friction states

Prioritize broken or slow screens around search, signup, account updates, assignment status, and report pages where users lose trust or leave the flow.

03

Unify data and release checks

Add clear ownership for status changes, regression coverage for portal-critical paths, and weekly demos so supplier or staffing changes stop breaking nearby journeys.

04

Leave you with a stable lane

Finish with a measured backlog, release checklist, and the next sprint scope for supplier reporting, worker accuracy, and client portal improvements.
