The weak point is servicing, not demand.

BrainFinance looks strong at fast access. The friction shows up in servicing: slower reviews, broken signing, unclear payment updates, and weak support when something goes wrong. That is a retention problem before it becomes a growth problem.

What We'd Do

Build a small delivery track around the borrower moments that trigger churn.

We would keep this practical: fix the risky moments, give ops better visibility, and add release discipline around lending workflows.

Repair the moments that break trust

Map the path from approval to repayment, then fix contract signing, amount selection, payment status, and re-borrow rules inside one mobile journey.

Give retention and support one operating view

Create a lean internal console for transfer status, payment exceptions, and outreach triggers so support, retention, and product share the same facts.

Ship with tighter fintech QA

Add coverage around bank-linking, repayment logic, and regressions so each release lowers support volume instead of moving the problem elsewhere.

Good pilot shape

A focused 6 to 8 week sprint with product, backend, mobile, QA, and business analysis. The goal is one cleaner servicing flow, not a rewrite.
