Public evidence behind the prototype

The strongest pattern is not "Sentry lacks features." It is that too much raw information still leaks into the release-decision moment.

Pain point 01 Noise is turning into spend

A Next.js user said client-side noise was driving Sentry costs up even with a small sampling rate. That is a signal-design problem, not a monitoring-coverage problem.

Pain point 02 Mobile can feel heavy

React Native feedback points to perceived slowdowns and the need for more careful tuning when Sentry gets added to mobile apps.

Pain point 03 Grouping trust is fragile

One of the clearest public complaints is that different errors can end up under one issue, which makes triage slower and explanations weaker.

Why now Execution is already a leadership topic

Sentry is hiring a Head of Technical Program Management reporting to the CTO, which reinforces that release predictability is strategically important.

Best product bet: do not add another dashboard. Add a release-aware layer that suppresses low-value client noise, highlights true regression risk, explains grouping confidence, and gives Solutions Engineering a cleaner story to tell.

What the prototype changes

This is intentionally narrow. It sits on top of Sentry's existing release-health and mobile-debug pipeline instead of trying to redesign the whole platform.

Feature 01 Release-aware suppression

Known low-value client noise is hidden from the critical release view while staying available in the full feed for later inspection.

Feature 02 Grouping confidence

Every cluster shows why it exists, what signals changed, and whether the issue is likely a true regression or a blended bucket.

Feature 03 Owner-ready next steps

Instead of a generic severity label, the card suggests the likely owner, the missing artifact, and the next operational move.

Feature 04 One decision language

Keep shipping, watch closely, or halt rollout. Simple language makes the product easier to trust and easier to demo.
