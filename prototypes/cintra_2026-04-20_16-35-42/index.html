Proposal Brief For Eric Dunmore

Help Cintra People scale without more admin drag

Cintra already sells one system for people and pay. Now the product has to remove the admin drag around reports, changes, approvals, and handoffs.

We saw Cintra is adding HR Administrator capacity. That often means routine people work is rising faster than the platform can absorb.

Product: Cintra People Focus: Reports, self-service, approvals Model: Dedicated product squad
Why We're Here

Our Read

The signal says admin load is climbing. Public pages show Cintra People becoming the single platform for payroll, HR, and people data. Reviews praise support and ease, but still flag hard reporting and awkward pay changes. Some tasks still need uploads or manual fixes after each payroll cycle. That means more admin work when the platform should be taking it away.

What We'd Do

A six-week delivery shape that stays close to the product

We would start with a dedicated squad under your product owner. The goal is simple: remove workflow friction that still pushes routine work back to admin and service teams.

Engagement Model

Dedicated squad under your product owner. Weekly demos. Clear backlog control stays with Cintra.

1

Map the true admin hotspots

Map the highest-friction admin journeys inside Cintra People. Turn them into a short, ranked backlog for reports, approvals, and employee data changes.

2

Cut manual payroll support steps

Remove spreadsheet steps where the product can handle the work. Start with pay-rate edits, unpaid hours, and report exports.

3

Lift manager self-service

Strengthen manager self-service for leave, documents, and approvals. Keep more routine work inside the platform and off support queues.

4

Protect migrations and integrations

Add API and QA guardrails around old product handoffs. Speed up migrations without risking live payroll cycles.

5

Finish with a usable next build list

Ship weekly demos, test notes, and clear next decisions. End the pilot with delivered fixes and the next build list.
