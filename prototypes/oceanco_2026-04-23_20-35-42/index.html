Proposal Brief Prepared for Martijn Dirkse, IT Service Manager

A steadier service layer for Oceanco's facility and outfit work

Oceanco is scaling work around facilities, outfitting, and life cycle support. That growth needs calmer request flow, better ownership, and clearer reporting across Alblasserdam, Zwijndrecht, and co-maker teams.

Why we're here

We built this brief after seeing two current roles. Oceanco is hiring a Senior Facility Engineer and a Lead Engineer - Outfit. That usually means more requests, more interfaces, and more pressure behind the yachts.

Our Read

The signal says Oceanco is adding capacity around facilities and outfit delivery. Public role pages ask for KPI reporting, HVAC know-how, interface control, and clean data flow. At the same time, Oceanco talks about digitalization, smart yachts, and digital twins. That mix often means the vision is clear, but the daily workflow is still manual. If that stays messy, response times slow and teams chase context.

What We'd Do

A six-week service pilot

We would start with a dedicated squad under Martijn's service ownership.

  • Team 1 delivery lead, 1 solution architect, 2 full-stack engineers, 1 QA engineer
  • Pilot shape Weekly demos, one live service flow, and a practical handover plan
  • Relevant stack React, .NET, Node.js, C#, QA automation, DevOps
  • 1 Request intake
  • 2 Triage and ownership
  • 3 Execution with co-makers
  • 4 SLA and KPI reporting

What lands in the pilot

  • 01
    Map the flow

    Map facility requests, vendor touchpoints, and outfit dependencies across both sites. Agree one service taxonomy and clear owners before building anything.

  • 02
    Unify the queue

    Set up one request hub with SLA states, handoff history, and simple dashboards. Martijn's team gets one live view of status and blockers.

  • 03
    Connect the sources

    Connect the first data sources around email, forms, spreadsheets, and project exports. Remove double entry where teams lose time today.

  • 04
    Launch safely

    Run the pilot with weekly demos and hard edge-case testing. Then prepare the next rollout for Life Cycle Support or refit work.
