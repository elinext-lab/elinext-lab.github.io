Why This Angle Works

The pitch is not “more AI.” It is better execution after the shortlist exists.

01

Public trust signals are fragile

Reddit comments about EVONA mention missed calls, unclear legitimacy, and no-response moments. A visible follow-up layer directly reduces that trust gap.

02

EVONA already claims shortlist intelligence

FRACTION publicly promotes AI, confidential shortlisting, direct interviews, and investor feedback. The next win is operational state, not another sourcing promise.

03

Volume makes follow-up a conversion problem

When public signals point to 119 applicants per position per month, the placement bottleneck becomes speed, ownership, and candidate confidence after shortlisting.

Research Basis

Built on EVONA's real public product footprint

This concept stays close to what EVONA already ships: FRACTION for search and shortlists, plus separate candidate and jobs surfaces. The gap is what happens between “good profile found” and “interview locked.”

FRACTION publicly says clients can browse and shortlist confidentially, set meetings directly, and share shortlists with colleagues and investors. The prototype simply turns those states into an operator-friendly mobile workflow.

That is why the right intervention for Richard Joyce is a contractor-services command layer, not a net-new discovery interface.
