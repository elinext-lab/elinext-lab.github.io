Our read

Premium sound raises the bar for service

The hiring signal says Devialet is trying to make customer operations more repeatable across outsourced support, reseller requests, and product escalations. Public reviews show why that matters now: people praise the sound, then complain about setup, pairing, stereo sync, and slow follow-up. When premium devices need resets or workarounds, the support team absorbs the gap. If that gap stays open, five-star trust gets tested by software and service, not by acoustics.

What we'd do

Ship one service layer that cuts repeat work

We would run this as a scoped end-to-end project. In eight weeks, we would ship a live workflow with weekly demos and one Devialet owner.

01

Map the real support path

Trace the path from app issue to outsourced ticket, partner case, and product escalation. Remove duplicate fields and unclear handoffs.

02

Build one triage model

Route B2C, B2B, and reseller requests by product, market, issue type, and SLA risk so agents stop guessing.

03

Turn repeat pain into guided playbooks

Create shared playbooks for setup, pairing, stereo sync, HDMI, and source switching, then reuse them in the knowledge base and FAQ.

04

Close the product feedback loop

Send one weekly issue view to product teams so release notes, agent scripts, and troubleshooting content move together.
