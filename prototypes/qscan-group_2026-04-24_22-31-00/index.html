Proposal brief for Peter Thornton

Helping Qscan steady doctor services across every referrer touchpoint

Qscan already promises premium referrer support, secure results, and easy e-Referrals. The harder step is keeping that experience steady. That gets harder as doctor services scale across more clinics and systems.

We saw Qscan is hiring a Head of Medical Services Operations. That usually means growth is outpacing the systems behind access, support, and clinic handoffs.

Primary product Qscan Referrer Results Secure web, software, and app access for images and reports to referring practitioners.
Project focus Doctor services flow Access status, report delivery, support queues, and stronger control across clinic handoffs.

Our read

The hiring signal suggests Qscan is scaling doctor services across a larger network. Public pages show many access paths for reports, referrals, and support. They include Referrer Results, practice software, Medical Objects, HealthLink, InteleViewer, and support lines. That helps growth, but it also spreads work across more handoffs. If that stays loose, premium referrer support gets harder to deliver everywhere.

What we'd do

Engagement model: a scoped 10-week project led with Peter's team. The goal is simple: make doctor services easier to run and easier to trust.

Duration
10 weeks

Weekly demos, one owner, and a clear finish line.

Team shape
1 product lead, 1 tech lead, 2 senior engineers, 1 data engineer, 1 QA

One team that can map, build, test, and hand over the service flow.

Stack
React, Node.js, Python, .NET, C#

A practical stack for portals, integrations, and service tooling.

01

Map the live service chain

Map doctor onboarding, Referrer Results, e-Referrals, and support queues. Find where access, ownership, and report delivery slow the day.

02

Build one operations view

Build one operations view for clinic requests, access status, blocked users, and escalations. Peter's team can spot risk before doctors call support.

03

Tighten access handoffs

Tighten rules across the portal, practice systems, and secure messaging. Cut repeated resets, unclear ownership, and manual follow-up between teams.

04

Stop small faults early

Add release checks, test coverage, and weekly service reviews. Small digital faults stop reaching Doctor Support and turning into clinic work.
