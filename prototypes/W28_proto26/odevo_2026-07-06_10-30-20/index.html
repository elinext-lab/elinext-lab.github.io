Helping Odevo ship AI field workflows with steadier delivery

Why we're here: Saw Odevo hiring for its AI Team while Upkept Assets is still young in the stores. This brief focuses on delivery work that protects field teams, not a fake redesign.

Prepared for Sermed Ali, Engineering Manager. The focus is AI delivery, mobile reliability, and the platform links behind property operations.

Our read

Your signal says Odevo is moving AI from experiments into product work. At the same time, Upkept Assets is new in the stores. Its release notes name Dispatch, offline use, longer sessions, and work order cards. That points to a delivery bottleneck across AI, mobile reliability, and platform links. If those tracks split, field teams get features that fail in weak networks. Sermed's focus on throughput fits this exact point.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

We would first map the Upkept Assets offline path: open Dispatch, edit a work order, add a photo, lose signal, then sync.

Useful next step

The next step is a release gate for retries, duplicate work orders, stuck photos, and long sessions. We would also pick one AI workflow, likely invoice handling or work order triage, and test it with source links and human approval.

What we'd do

Engagement model: a dedicated squad under Odevo's PO/PM, with weekly demos and clear release gates.

Team shape: one tech lead, two AI/backend engineers, one mobile engineer, and one QA automation engineer.

Pilot: six weeks, then extend only if the metrics help Sermed's team.
