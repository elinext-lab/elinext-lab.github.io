Why this product direction is defensible

Forsyth Barnes does not need a generic consumer app. It needs a mobile layer for recurring candidate behavior: checking status, preparing for the next step, and staying warm during slow client review windows.

Website promise The portal already sells alerts, saved profiles, and application history.

That means the business has already framed recruiting as a repeat product interaction, not just a one-time form submit.

Public signal Review snippets call out responsiveness and weak follow-through.

The trust issue is not top-of-funnel awareness. It is the silent period after interest becomes intent.

Growth context Utilities, renewable energy, telecom, and infrastructure roles increase process complexity.

Longer cycles and more stakeholders make a status-first mobile experience more valuable, not less.

Gap spotted Forsyth Barnes promises visibility. The prototype makes that promise tactile.
Why mobile Candidates check progress from phones long before they revisit a desktop portal.
Why now Specialist growth into energy and infrastructure raises the cost of every missed update.

What Elinext would actually build

Start with a PWA or hybrid mobile shell that sits on top of the current portal. The goal is fast delivery and measurable trust improvements, not a long native rewrite.

Module 01 Candidate timeline and status center

Every application gets a live stage, named owner, expected update date, and self-serve next action.

Module 02 Recruiter SLA and auto-follow-up

Trigger nudges when a role stalls, and let recruiters send approved progress notes in one tap instead of reactive email chasing.

Module 03 Content and application journey in one flow

Connect the salary guide and sector content to live candidate journeys so engagement keeps moving even while clients deliberate.

What this changes for the business

The payoff is practical: fewer anxious follow-up emails, better repeat mobile engagement, clearer recruiter accountability, and a stronger product story for a firm that already positions itself around AI and technology.
