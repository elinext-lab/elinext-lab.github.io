We saw Leeds is hiring an Information Technology Operations Manager for a client. The role names ERP migration, SaaS transition, Azure, MSP models, and multi-site operations.

Our read

Leeds already sells a high-touch process: meet the client, find passive candidates, pre-qualify face to face, guide interviews, offers, checks, and resignation. The IT Operations Manager search raises the bar. A Dynamics NAV to Business Central move, Azure work, MSP governance, and multi-site support need clear evidence at each handoff. If updates live in email and one contact form, a strong candidate can move slowly. The risk is not only missing a role. It is losing trust when a complex client needs fast, clean answers.

Where we'd start

Intake branch

Your contact form asks "Business" or "Prospective Candidate," but both paths share one intake. Split them after the first choice and ask business leads for ERP, site count, urgency, and decision team.

Status path

Your clients page lists a strong process, but clients do not see its status. Add a light portal for shortlist stage, interview feedback, checks, and next action.
