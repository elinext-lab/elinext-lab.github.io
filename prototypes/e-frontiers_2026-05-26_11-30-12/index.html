Proposal brief by Elinext

E-Frontiers can move faster on tech hiring

A focused web squad can make the job board faster, clearer, and easier for specialist engineers to trust.

Saw the new Elixir fullstack role in Madrid. It points to a product-company search need with AWS, ElasticSearch, microservices, and front-end work in one hire.

Why we are here

Your public job board is part of that search. It must feel fast, clear, and safe before an engineer sends a CV.

Our read

The role says your Spain team is covering more hard product searches, not only routine hiring. Candidates need clear detail, fast filters, and trust before they send a CV. Your public job board already has strong reach, but it carries heavy WordPress code, old scripts, and many form steps. If that stays as demand grows, recruiters may lose good engineers before the first call.

What we would do

Engagement model: a dedicated squad under Brendan or the site owner, with weekly demos.

Team shape: 1 lead engineer, 1 WordPress/PHP engineer, 1 front-end engineer, 1 QA, 1 UX designer.

Duration: six-week pilot, then optional monthly support.

Relevant stack

PHP JavaScript Node.js Python QA automation

Map the candidate path for Madrid tech roles, from search filters to CV upload and recruiter contact.

Improve forms for candidates and employers, with fewer fields, clearer errors, and safer file upload checks.

Elinext is a full-cycle software development company, working since 1997 with about 700 in-house specialists and no freelancers. For E-Frontiers, the useful part is not a big rebuild; it is a steady team that can improve a live hiring site without slowing recruiters.
