Backend talent demand is testing Gravitas' digital hiring flow

We saw the Back End Developer search from Gravitas. It points to a hard specialist role, where fast candidate capture and clean client intake both matter.

Our read

The signal says Gravitas is carrying high-value backend searches, not basic CV volume. The public listing asks for TypeScript, Node.js, infrastructure, CI/CD, observability, and ownership. That is a narrow candidate path. On the Gravitas site, the job search, CV upload, and Submit a Job flow are the main product moments. If those flows do not capture intent cleanly, consultants carry more manual follow-up. The risk is slower matches while demand for specialist technology talent stays high.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

The jobs page can show an empty result state. Keep related technology roles, CV registration, and job alerts visible there, so good candidates still move forward.

Useful next step

The client page promises scoping, salary benchmarking, and market intelligence. Turn that promise into a guided job brief that routes cleaner data to consultants.

What we'd do

Use a six-week dedicated squad under your product or operations owner: one backend lead, one full-stack engineer, one data integration engineer, and one QA automation engineer.

1

Map the live flows

Trace jobs, CV upload, client intake, HubSpot, and analytics events around hard backend roles.

2

Fix empty states

Design expired-role paths with related jobs, backend skill capture, and clear consultant follow-up.

3

Improve client intake

4

Prove it weekly

Ship in small demos, with regression tests for mobile search, forms, and tracking.
