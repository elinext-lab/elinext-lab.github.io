Prepared for Skybound Games

Help Skybound keep new games moving after launch

A short, practical brief on how an added engineering squad could support game production, live fixes, partner delivery, and fan trust across Skybound Games now.

We saw the Senior Producer (Games) hiring signal. It points to more titles, more external teams, and more launch pressure around Skybound IP.

Our read

Hiring a Senior Producer for Games says Skybound is adding production control around live game work, not just making more content. Invincible VS is already in its first post-launch window, with public patches covering save data loss, ranked disconnects, motion inputs, crashes, and balance loops. That is normal for a new fighting game, but it needs fast triage because the community decides how long a title keeps support. Glenn's TV team also depends on the same IP trust: fans punish gaps between story promise and product feel.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

Turn the May and June patch notes into a public issue map: save data, ranked disconnects, motion inputs, and crash paths each need an owner, test matrix, and next player note.

Useful next step

Use Reddit roster and genre doubts as a content backlog, so tutorials, character spotlights, and DLC timing answer the exact fears fans name before they harden.

What we'd do

Use a dedicated squad under Skybound's producer or PM. Keep creative control inside Skybound, while Elinext handles release support, QA, and partner follow-through.
