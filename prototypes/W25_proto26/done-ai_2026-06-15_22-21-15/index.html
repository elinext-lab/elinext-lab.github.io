Done.ai brief

Keep Done OS moving while the full-stack team scales

A short engineering brief for Staffan Herbst, focused on the hiring signal for a Full Stack Developer and the public rollout of Done CRM inside Done OS.

Saw Done.ai is hiring full-stack help while Done CRM has opened as the first native Done OS app. That points to real product delivery load, not a generic resourcing gap.

Our read

Your hiring signal says Done.ai is adding full-stack capacity while Done CRM is now live. That means the product work is moving from launch to scale. The hard part is not one more screen. It is keeping CRM, accounting, POS, MRP, and recruiting on one data model. Your own site promises one login and one source of truth for Nordic SMEs. If each module ships its own patterns, onboarding slows and integration debt grows.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

app.done.ai already separates identity, API, and analytics. Add contract tests around those joins before more Done OS modules depend on them.

Useful next step

Lighthouse flagged unnamed public links and missing app-entry metadata. Fix those small launch details before wider CRM invites scale.

What we'd do

Run a six-week dedicated squad under your Product Owner. Team shape: one tech lead, two senior full-stack engineers, one QA automation engineer, and one UX designer.
