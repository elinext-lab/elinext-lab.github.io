Our read

Togetherwork Arts is not short on product direction. The signal says the hard part is turning that direction into shipped, stable work. Acceptd already spans applications, media upload, scheduling, adjudication, payments, and applicant messages. When one flow changes, release risk can spread across applicants, reviewers, and support. If delivery stays thin, Laura's team may spend more time protecting launches than moving the roadmap.

Where we would start

Media flow

Acceptd support pages mention failed uploads, processing delays, and large files. Start with a deadline-week regression path for upload, retry, processing, submit, and status messaging.

Submitted changes

Changing a submitted application needs support or an Account Owner. Add guardrails for stage changes, reviewer locks, applicant notice, and deadline bypass before the next release.

What we would do

Use a dedicated squad under your Product or PM lead for a six-week pilot. Team: one technical lead, two full-stack engineers, one QA automation engineer, and one product data analyst.
