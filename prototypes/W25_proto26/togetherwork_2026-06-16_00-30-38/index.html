Our read

That Delivery Product Manager role tells us CircuiTree is moving from custom camp work into a steadier SaaS model. The hard part is not only roadmap choice. It is keeping registration, payments, cabin work, Bunk1 photos, and parent messages stable while teams say no to one-off asks. Public app reviews keep pointing to blank photo galleries, slow loads, missing facial matches, and confusion around paid notes. If this stays open through camp season, support load rises and product teams lose time fixing urgent cases.

Where we'd start

Photo and note path

Map every parent step from login to photo view and Bunk Note delivery. Add clear empty, failed, and delivered states before adding new features.

Season-ready product page

The CircuiTree camp page waits too long to settle on mobile. Tighten heavy media and forms so demo traffic gets a faster first view.

What we'd do

Use a dedicated squad under Togetherwork's Product and Engineering leads. Shape it as one Tech Lead, two senior full-stack engineers, one mobile engineer, one QA engineer, and part-time UX.
