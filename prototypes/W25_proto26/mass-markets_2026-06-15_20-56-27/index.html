Mass Markets: scale bilingual CX with cleaner delivery

Mass Markets already has the AI-CX platform story. The next gain is making bilingual support flows easier to launch, check, and improve.

Saw Mass Markets and MCI hiring many bilingual German, Portuguese, French, and Spanish call center roles. That usually means language coverage is growing faster than playbooks, QA, and support tooling.

Why this page exists A short software delivery plan for multilingual agent assist, QA, and integrations.

Our read

The signal says Mass Markets is scaling multilingual service delivery, not just filling seats. Public roles ask agents to handle inbound and outbound calls, translate clearly, update records, escalate issues, and meet daily goals. At the same time, AiCX sells AI agents, real-time agent assist, omnichannel orchestration, and quality coaching. The hard part is making those tools work across languages without slowing live operations. If playbooks, QA tags, and CRM notes differ by market, new agents need more support and managers lose clean data.

Where we'd start

Noticed

Language roles point to repeatable flows

Map each bilingual job language to live call reasons, escalation paths, and QA forms. That gives agent assist one source before more agents join.

Next step

Make the first buyer path lighter

Test the public AiCX path on mobile and simplify the first call-to-action route. Our audit saw a heavy first load before the platform story.
