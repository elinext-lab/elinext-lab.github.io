Our read

Togetherwork is scaling a wide SaaS portfolio, not one small app. The Delivery Manager role points to a need for tighter release flow across many product lines. Your jobs page names a camp management platform that covers staff recruitment, parent registration, payment plans, bunk assignments, and scheduling. Stripe's public story shows the same pattern: fast growth, many products, and hard payment integration work. If delivery ownership is late, teams can ship fixes, but key flows still slip across web, mobile, and payments.

Where we'd start

Booking flow

Recent Gingr and PetExec users report date errors, forced re-login, unreadable forms, and account mismatch. First step: create a shared booking regression pack for iOS, Android, and web.

Portfolio playbook

Stripe helped phase payment onboarding across products. Use that pattern for camp releases too: map the few high-risk flows, assign one owner, and gate each release.

What we'd do

Engagement model: a dedicated squad under your Product Owner or new Delivery Manager, not an outsourced product takeover.

Pilot: six weeks, weekly demos, clear handoff notes, and a reusable delivery playbook.
