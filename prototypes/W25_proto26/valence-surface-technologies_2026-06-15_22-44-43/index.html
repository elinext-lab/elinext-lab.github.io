Help planners turn specs into clean work orders faster

Saw Valence is hiring Planners while ProFlow and the approvals table carry a lot of spec-to-work-order detail. This brief shows where extra software capacity could cut planning load first.

Our read

Your Planner hiring signal points to one pressure: more customer specs must become correct work orders fast. Valence says the approvals search covers OEM, process, specification, and location across a very large approval list. The Seattle role also asks planners to verify purchase orders, maintain MRP data, and create certification documents. If that work stays too manual, planners become the bottleneck between customer demand, ProFlow updates, and on-time delivery.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

The approvals page asks buyers to search OEM, process, specification, or location. Add saved views for frequent checks, then track which searches create quote or planner handoffs.

Next useful step

Planner roles mention MRP updates, work orders, certification docs, and purchase-order checks. Map those fields once, then prototype a rules-based spec intake helper.

What we'd do

A dedicated squad under Valence's product owner or IT operations lead, run as a six-week pilot with weekly demos.
