Prepared for Austin Film Festival

Reduce festival admin load before the screener season peaks

A short software brief for Austin Film Festival, focused on submission review, schedule access, and the small digital gaps that can create long staff days.

We saw the screener hiring signal. That usually means more films, more volunteer coordination, and more pressure on the tools around the competition.

Why we are here The near-term risk is not a lack of ideas. It is the handoff load between submissions, screeners, schedules, venues, and attendee questions.

Our read

Bringing on volunteer film competition screeners means AFF is entering the heavy review window, not just filling a small role. The hard part is keeping submissions, screeners, schedule data, and filmmaker emails in sync while the core team protects the festival experience. AFF already sends people through FilmFreeway, its own submission pages, and Sched for the festival schedule. Public attendees also use Sched to judge lines and venue demand. If those handoffs stay manual, staff time goes to status checks and fixes instead of filmmakers, panelists, and guests.

Where we'd start

Noticed

The Sched mobile path asks users to find AFF, then sign up or log in. Recent Sched reviews mention sign-up and event-selection friction. Add a pre-event schedule check page before badge pickup.

Next step

The 2025 schedule spans many sessions and venues. Public attendees use Sched to plan lines. Mark high-demand screenings with clear line notes and staff-owned fallback links.

What we'd do

We would run a scoped end-to-end project, not staff leasing. The aim is one small operations layer that helps AFF staff before the busy months.
