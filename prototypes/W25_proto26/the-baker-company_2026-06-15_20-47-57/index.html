Tighten Baker's quote to engineering handoff

Saw Baker is hiring electrical engineers for biosafety cabinets. Here is how we would reduce manual handoffs before more custom work reaches operations.

Baker already has strong products, decision tools, a calculator, a parts store, and support depth. The next gain may be a cleaner software path between buyer input, engineering options, and production data.

Our read

The hiring signal says Baker is adding electrical engineering capacity around biosafety cabinets. That often means custom options, controls, test data, and documentation are moving faster than the current handoff can support. SterilGARD e3 already points buyers to a decision map and cost calculator, while Baker's site also routes quotes, parts, and service. If those paths stay separate, engineers spend more time clarifying requests. The risk is not only slower quotes. It is avoidable load on a team that must protect safety, quality, and delivery.

Where we'd start

What we noticed

The decision map, cost calculator, quote form, and parts store live as separate flows. Map one live SterilGARD request through each handoff, then remove repeated fields.

Useful next step

The SterilGARD mobile page ships heavy scripts and has named accessibility gaps. Defer non-critical widgets and add labels before a wider rebuild.

1 Map the handoff

Trace quote, calculator, parts, and support paths for SterilGARD and BioChemGARD requests, then mark each manual handoff.

2 Shape the intake

Build a simple engineering intake that captures options, electrical needs, missing data, and the owner for each answer.

3 Connect the systems

Link WordPress forms, Salesforce/Pardot data, and engineering notes into one request record that operations can review.

4 Test and hand over

Run mobile, accessibility, and workflow QA, then leave Baker with backlog-ready tickets and clear launch notes.
