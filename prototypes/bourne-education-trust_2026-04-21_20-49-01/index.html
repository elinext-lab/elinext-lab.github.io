Our Read

The role tells us BET is protecting delivery in a practical subject area. At the same time, BET Digital covers learning, teaching, and operations. That mix often leaves handover, access, and local support split across folders, emails, and team habits. If that stays manual, leaders lose time, new staff start slower, and trust plans land unevenly.

What We’d Do

We would run a scoped end-to-end project. The aim is one light operating layer around BET Digital, staffing continuity, and school support.

Team Shape 1 business analyst, 1 tech lead, 2 full-stack engineers, 1 QA engineer
Working Rhythm 6-week project, weekly demos, clear backlog, simple sign-off points
Relevant Stack React, .NET, Node.js, Python, QA automation

Project Focus

Build one practical layer that helps trust teams and schools work the same way.

Map the live workflow

Build one secure hub for schemes, cover notes, supplier details, and onboarding packs. Keep permissions simple for trust staff, department leads, and school teams.

Connect vacancy tasks, document handover, and first-week readiness steps. New leaders start with the right access, files, and local context.

Add clear usage views, support notes, and simple admin rules. Central teams can spot gaps without pulling schools into extra follow-up.
