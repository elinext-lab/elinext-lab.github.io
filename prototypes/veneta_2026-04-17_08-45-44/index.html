Visible pressure points

Our Read

Our read of it

The hiring signal says Veneta is strengthening the team that sits between customer, advisor, and installer. Your public flow already spans samples, online meethulp, home advice, order status, and after-sales support. When those handoffs drift, the contact team becomes the manual fix for remake cases, warranty questions, and missed callbacks. That slows replies, creates repeat contact, and puts a strong brand at risk during the hardest moments.

What We’d Do

A practical support plan

We would place a dedicated squad under your product or operations lead for an eight-week pilot, then extend only if it proves useful.

Team Shape

Lean squad for live workflow change
  • 1 tech lead to own the delivery path
  • 2 senior web engineers for workflow delivery
  • 1 business analyst for cases and rules
  • 1 QA engineer for regression and edge cases

Relevant Stack

Used where it fits your estate

We would keep the work close to your setup.

React Node.js .NET / C# QA automation DevOps

Pilot Milestones

Four steps with weekly demos
  • 01

    Map the live journey

    Map the full path from online meethulp to delivered order, so each case keeps one clear history across advice, planning, service, and installation.

  • 02

    Fix service ownership

    Tighten guarantee and remake handling with clear state changes, owner rules, and alerts for stalled callbacks, missing notes, or double-booked visits.

  • 03

    Support planners and installers

    Rework planner and service screens so advisors and installers share the same appointment context, product detail, and promise made to the customer.

  • 04

    Track repeat-contact risk

    Add weekly QA checks around photo upload, order-status updates, and post-purchase messages, then show the new manager where repeat contact starts.
