AI-Powered Mobile Teardown
TribolaTech x 24x7 Managed Operations

What if 2am alerts came with a guided mobile incident bridge?

TribolaTech already sells 24x7 monitoring, ServiceNow AI, and proactive incident response. The gap is the last mile: when a power event or P1 database outage hits, responders still need one place to see CI context, trust the data, launch the runbook, and pull the right owner in fast.

The prototype angle

We designed Ops Pulse, a mobile incident commander for TribolaTech's DBA and ServiceNow-led operations. It focuses on the exact friction visible in the public material: gaps during nights and weekends, elevated MTTR, thin self-service, and too much context trapped inside dashboards and SOPs.

Why This Works

A realistic software wedge for a services-heavy story

This concept does not ask TribolaTech to become a consumer-app company. It packages the most valuable part of its managed-services promise into a tighter operator experience: faster acknowledgment, better context, more reliable escalation, and a cleaner ServiceNow handoff.

For Vishal, that means a stronger demo story, a more differentiated reliability offer, and a mobile layer that supports both internal teams and client-facing operations.

Aligned to public pain

TribolaTech's own ops content already names support gaps, elevated MTTR, and thin tooling adoption. The prototype attacks that exact bottleneck.

Aligned to CRM pain context

Power-outage monitoring, infrastructure visibility, and AIOps self-service are already in the pre-collected lead context, so the concept is not a generic mobile pitch.

Small enough to execute

A mobile incident commander can be shipped as a focused product surface that sits on top of ServiceNow, observability, and runbook data instead of replacing existing systems.

TribolaTech already has the data and delivery model. The next step is a better moment of response.
