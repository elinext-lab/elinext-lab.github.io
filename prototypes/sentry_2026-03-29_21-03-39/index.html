App Store and Play search did not surface an official Sentry companion. That makes mobile incident response an open product wedge rather than a redesign request.

Public feedback

Powerful, but noisy

Reddit discussions around Sentry alternatives call out expense and information overload. The right answer is not less capability. It is better signal compression.

Roadmap fit

AI plus delivery discipline

Seer is already live, and Sentry is hiring TPM leadership to improve execution visibility. A mobile decision layer amplifies both trends without bloating the core web product.

Why This Fits

Indragie’s public profile makes this feel unusually well matched.

The CRM record makes AI the main current lane. Publicly, Indragie also frames his background around iOS, mobile infrastructure, observability, and a mobile APM company that Sentry acquired. That is exactly the mix needed for a mobile AI workflow that feels product-native rather than bolted on.

The concept does not try to shrink the whole Sentry UI onto a phone. It turns Seer into a decision surface: compress, rank, explain, act, and move.

Signal 01

Seer’s public GA metrics prove Sentry already believes AI should reduce debugging effort, not just generate text.

Signal 02

The careers page says Sentry wants high-signal delivery visibility and fewer surprises across EPD. A mobile approval lane supports that operating model.

Signal 03

Mobile workflows are strategic, from crash reporting to app-size intelligence, but the public interaction surface still looks desktop-first.
