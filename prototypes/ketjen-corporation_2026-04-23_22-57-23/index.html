Our read

That hiring move suggests the FCC team is adding capacity at the plant-data edge. At the same time, Ketjen promotes Digital Services, predictive insights, and an E-Cat portal built for faster access to results and trends. The hard part is not collecting more data. The hard part is keeping portal logic, models, and customer reporting in step while engineering work grows. If that gap stays, your experts spend time stitching context together and customers wait longer for clear guidance.

What we'd do

We would start with a dedicated squad under Ketjen's technical owner. The pilot stays narrow and works on the live E-Cat portal plus the data services behind it.

1

Map the flow

Trace FCC sample and plant-signal data into portal views, then mark slow handoffs, manual checks, and duplicated report paths.

2

Stabilize core portal paths

Harden results, trends, and cross-plot journeys, then add regression tests before new Digital Services changes reach refinery customers.

3

Split risky logic

Move fragile benchmark, alert, and derived-value rules into cleaner services so portal releases stop breaking analysis views.

4

Add release control

Set weekly demos, release notes, and basic telemetry so Ketjen sees where users still leave the portal for email or spreadsheets.
